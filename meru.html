ABOUT MOUNT MERU

The second-highest mountain in Tanzania after Kilimanjaro is Mount Meru, which is situated within the Arusha National Park and provides views of Arusha town. Climbing that peak is thrilling. On the lower slopes, there are many creatures to see, and as you climb, different plant zones appear.

The views of Kilimanjaro, which is 80 kilometers to the northeast, and the cliff face that descends from the peak to Meru Crater are stunning, despite some very steep sections of the trek along the saddle. However, no specialized gear is required for the ascent.

Since it takes roughly 7 hours to descend from the summit of Mount Meru, some climbers choose to add an extra night in Miriakamba Hut, making the climb take four days and three nights. On the way up and down, there are mountain lodges where you can spend the night.

MOUNT MERU - CLIMBING DISCRIPTION

Day ONE

You are driven to Momela Gate (1500 meters) in the morning to begin the ascent; it takes 3 hours to reach Mariakamba Hut. Explore the Meru Crater in the afternoon. At Mariakamba Hut, spend the night.

DAY TWO

It will take roughly three hours to ascend the steep Saddle cliffs to Saddle Hut. In the afternoon, climb Little Meru (3820m) for one and a half hours. Spend the night at Saddle Hut.

DAY THREE

Spend around three hours ascending the steep Saddle cliffs to reach Saddle Hut. In the afternoon, climb Little Meru, which is 3820 meters high. Saddle Hut, overnight

Mt. Meru can be reached through Little Meru, despite the fact that Olkokola on its western side is where it is typically climbed. Although there may be some rain in November, the months of October through February are the ideal times to climb the peak.
