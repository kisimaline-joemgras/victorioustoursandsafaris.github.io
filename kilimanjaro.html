ABOUT MOUNT KILIMANJARO

The highest mountain in Africa and the highest free-standing mountain in the world, Mount Kilimanjaro (5895 meters), may be found in Tanzania. Despite its height, Kilimanjaro is one of the world's most accessible high mountains, with most climbers needing only a walking stick, appropriate clothes, and willpower to reach the top (Uhuru Peak).

From the tropical rain forest on the lower slopes and the alpine heath to an almost naked alpine desert and eventually the wintry Arctic top of snow and ice, just 3 degrees south of the Equator, climbing Mount Kilimanjaro is like taking a virtual climate tour of the world.

There are six ways to climb Kilimanjaro: the Mweka, Umbwe, Shira, Rongai, Machame, and the simpler Marangu Route. The length of the Kilimanjaro climb might range from four to six nights on the mountain, depending on the route chosen. The Marangu and Machame routes are the two busiest. While camping is the only form of lodging on the Machame Route, alpine lodges are used on the Marangu Route. There is no need for technical equipment on these routes, and you will be accompanied on your climb by knowledgeable guides and porters.

MOUNT KILIMANJARO - ROUTES DISCRIPTION

MACHAME ROUTE

This is most likely the most beautiful ascent of Kilimanjaro. The only available lodging on the ascent and descent is camping (mobile tents). Because of its difficulty, this climb may be best suited to more experienced hikers. Normal completion times for the Machame route on the mountain are at least 6 days and 5 nights.

MARANGU ROUTE

The most common path to the summit is by far the Marangu, or main route. There is no need for special equipment, and the rise is more gradual. Accommodations on the ascent and descent are offered in mountain huts equipped with all the amenities. At Mandara Hut (2700m) and Kibo Hut (4700m), there are each 60 bunk beds (for 60 people each), and at Horombo Hut (3720m), which is also used for descent, there are 120 bunk beds (for 120 people). Sleeping bags should be brought with you even if mattresses and pillows are provided for climbers. There are restrooms, washrooms, and toilets shared by everyone.

Usually, the round trip takes 5 days and 4 nights. The trek can also be completed in 6 days and 5 nights with an additional day spent at Horombo Hut to allow for acclimatization. An additional fee for an additional day is listed individually as an option.

RONGAI ROUTE

On the mountain's northeastern slope, there is just one route, Rongai, which typically requires five nights to complete. It may not be quite as spectacular as the western routes, but this less popular route sees significantly less traffic than the other five night routes. By approaching Kilimanjaro from the north, this path preserves a sense of untamed wildness and provides a new viewpoint of the mountain. It is typically regarded as the most straightforward of the mountain's quality routes. For those seeking a fantastic experience but who may not be completely secure in their fitness, Rongai is the perfect option.

UMBWE ROUTE

The Umbwe route, headquartered in the south, is a 6-day, 5-night excursion that is regarded as being both the most beautiful and challenging one available on Kilimanjaro. Only experienced mountaineers should attempt the first two days because they are incredibly difficult, muddy, and steep. The climber can add an acclimatization day to the usual itinerary, which would give them an additional day in the Karanga Valley. The Mweka Route's descent trail is the same.

LEMOSHO ROUTE

The Lemosho Glades path on Mount Kilimanjaro is possibly the least popular due of its remote position and the challenging roads that get to the trailhead. Only 4-wheel drive vehicles can travel the route to the trailhead, and it can become inaccessible during wet weather. Due to the relatively high height of the trailhead, we begin this trek slowly and carefully. On the first day of a forest trip, it's possible to see buffalo and elephants, and the track is sometimes overgrown from infrequent use.

Fitness undoubtedly plays a role in the fun and success of this climb because it is the longest way up Kilimanjaro. On the mountain, the Lemosho (Shira) path requires 7 days and 6 nights. Overall, this walk is only recommended for experienced hikers due to the distance traveled and the difficulty of the final several days. This walk is especially memorable due to the numerous scenery changes and breathtaking hike across the Shira Plateau. On this route, there are no cabins; instead, travelers stay in mountain tents.
